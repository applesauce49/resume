Summary

At Intel, I built a career at the intersection of security, compliance, and innovation—translating complex policies and technologies into practical, scalable systems that empowered engineers and researchers to move faster with confidence. I’ve led initiatives spanning secure development, open-source governance, Responsible AI, and modernization of legacy toolchains—earning a reputation as a trusted bridge between Legal, Executive, and Engineering teams. Whether driving a full compliance process redesign, architecting cross-platform SDKs, or mentoring teams through high-stakes product releases, I combine technical depth with organizational empathy to turn complexity into clarity and execution.

Key Achievements

  • Transformed Intel Labs’ software compliance ecosystem, cutting release timelines from weeks to hours and creating the Intel Labs Compliance Portal, a company-wide knowledge hub for security and open-source best practices.
  • Pioneered Responsible AI and open-source model governance, enabling Intel’s first multimodal AI model release (LAVA, derived from Meta’s Llama 3) through alignment across Legal, Research, and Executive stakeholders.
  • Modernized enterprise development practices by leading GitHub migration onboarding, developer training, and process integration for hundreds of repositories across global research teams.
  • Re-architected the Adaptive Contextual Aware Toolkit (ACAT) accessibility suite, boosting test coverage from 0 % → 85 % and delivering the first new feature release in two years.
  • Strengthened Intel’s security posture through code audits, secure compiler and coding standards, cross-geo coordination, and development of a reusable SDL testing framework.
  • Delivered security training and mentorship, including Introduction to Security Principles and Cryptography and hands-on fuzzing projects with university interns.
  • Collaborated with the U.S. Air Force Academy’s Center of Innovation on secure collaboration software using Intel SGX—co-presenting findings to faculty and DHS representatives.
  • Resolved critical vulnerabilities in collaboration with Intel PSIRT, driving patches and institutionalizing secure coding practices across teams.
